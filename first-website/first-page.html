Here is my Autobiographical Poem

The sea from a distance, Gleaming, proud of the sun. In a land surrounded by waves, Lies a wave surrounded by land. “Hello, fair ocean”, he beckoned - so far away, A boy never felt the tides. Soft grass and course cane, all around. Casuarinas weep, at the wind now dry, Its mist returned to the clouds. An emerald sea, yet sapphire he longed for, Turned a sullen gray. Geometric skylines border his new world, Asphalt smells of change. Titans of glass and steel, Rivers of their stone blood lead deep underground. A road, a track, Away from the sea, Now, he will even take green.
